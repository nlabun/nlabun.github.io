Leadership and culture

Organizational culture is manifested in several ways, perhaps the most tangible and direct being statements of mission, purpose, or vision. These are the espoused values of an organization – "the desired culture" – which can often be in conflict with the enacted values lived and experienced by members of the organization. This quick-and-dirty analysis is intended to explore commonalities in espoused values across large, high-performing organizations…and maybe across notable defunct organizations as well.

Methodology This mostly-unscientific analysis identifies how frequently common adjectives and nouns appear across published statements of mission, purpose, vision, or values of 2023 Fortune 30 companies, as well as records of the same statements from notable defunct companies. A corpus of these statements was created by manually scraping of relevant active or archived web pages for each company, and analyzed with NLP in Python to measure frequency of co-occurence of nouns and adjectives across companies. Note that this approach does not account for semantic word/phrase meaning and is limited by minimal data cleaning of the corpus (such as filtering of adverbs and pronouns) and limited text datasets for both "successful" and "failed" companies. | Sources & credits All content on organizational culture courtesy of Professor Nate Pettit. The text corpus for this project was sourced from web pages for each respective company, either via live website or an archived version made available by Internet Archive.
